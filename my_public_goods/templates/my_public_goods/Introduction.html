Please read the following instructions carefully:

  • Today you will be playing a public goods game. You will play a total of 12 rounds.
  • You have been randomly assigned to a group and will remain a part of this group for the duration of the experiment. Each group has {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Members of your group are distributed throughout the room and will remain anonymous for the entirety of the experiment.
  • In each round, you will be given an endowment of {{ Constants.endowment }} and will have opportunity to contribute from that endowment to the group.
  • Once all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} members of your group have decided how much to contribute, the sum of contributions to the group will be multiplied by {{ Constants.efficiency_factor }} and divided evenly among all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} group members.
  • Next, each group member will have the chance to sanction other group members for their lack of contribution to the group. A list of contributions will be displayed in a random order so that it will never be possible to determine how much each group member contributed. It costs {{ Constants.sanction_fee }} to sanction another group member, and the group member being sanctioned will receive a penalty of {{ Constants.sanction_penalty }}.
  • At the end of this experiment, one round will be selected and participants will be paid the amount that they ended that round with (in addition to the $5 show-up fee.

When you have finished reading the instructions, you may advance to the next page. You will be asked to answer a few questions to confirm that you understand the rules of the game.
